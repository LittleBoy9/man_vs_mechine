Field Notes from the Interface

Three blog-style entries about where human language still matters most: editing, memory, and the politics of style.

Essay 01

Editing is still a human art

An AI draft can create options, but editing is where responsibility begins. The editor decides tone, stakes, omissions, and what should never have been written in the first place.

Essay 02

Archives need context, not just OCR

Turning everything into searchable text does not create understanding. Context arrives through curation, sequencing, footnotes, and contradiction.

Essay 03

Style is not a skin

When systems imitate voice at scale, style stops being a decorative layer and becomes a site of labor politics, ownership, and taste.

Reading note

The danger is not that machines write. It is that people stop reading carefully.

Most interface harm happens when plausible text gets routed into trusted spaces without a human slowdown phase. This page is designed to argue for that slowdown.
